# Company Research Guide

## Overview
This guide provides a structured approach to researching target companies for interview preparation, focusing on understanding their products, business model, and culture.

## Research Framework

### 1. Company Basics
- **Company Name & Industry**: Full name, sector, and market position
- **Founded**: Year established and founding story
- **Size**: Employee count, offices, market presence
- **Stage**: Startup (Series A/B/C), scale-up, established enterprise
- **Funding**: Recent funding rounds, investors, valuation

### 2. Product & Business Analysis
- **Core Products/Services**: What they build and sell
- **Target Customers**: B2B, B2C, B2B2C - who pays for the product
- **Value Proposition**: What problem they solve and how
- **Business Model**: Revenue streams (SaaS, marketplace, ads, etc.)
- **Technology Stack**: Key technologies and platforms used
- **Competitive Advantage**: What makes them unique

### 3. Recent Developments
- **News & Updates**: Last 6 months of significant announcements
- **Product Launches**: New features, products, or services
- **Strategic Initiatives**: Expansions, partnerships, acquisitions
- **Challenges**: Known issues, layoffs, pivots, market changes

### 4. Culture & Values
- **Mission & Vision**: Stated purpose and long-term goals
- **Core Values**: Stated company values and how they manifest
- **Work Culture**: Remote/hybrid/office, pace, team structure
- **Employee Reviews**: Glassdoor, Blind - common themes

### 5. Team & Leadership
- **Key Executives**: CEO, CTO, VPs - their backgrounds
- **Team Structure**: How the engineering/product teams are organized
- **Hiring Patterns**: Growth areas, common backgrounds of hires

## Information Sources

### Primary Sources
- **Company Website**: About, blog, press releases, careers page
- **Product**: Try the product yourself if possible
- **LinkedIn**: Company page, employee profiles, job postings
- **GitHub**: Open source contributions, tech stack clues

### Secondary Sources
- **News Sites**: TechCrunch, The Information, Bloomberg
- **Crunchbase**: Funding, investors, key metrics
- **Glassdoor/Blind**: Employee perspectives and reviews
- **Industry Reports**: Gartner, Forrester for established companies

## Tips

1. **Start Broad, Go Deep**: Begin with overview sources, then dive into specifics
2. **Verify Information**: Cross-reference facts from multiple sources
3. **Stay Current**: Focus on last 6-12 months for most relevant context
4. **Think Like an Insider**: Understand their challenges and priorities
5. **Connect to Role**: Always relate findings back to the position you're applying for
